Abstract

Two-track evidence: method power, then paper protocol

This report separates the evidence into two tracks. The first track is a method-power study: keep DINOv2, CLIP, and SigLIP frozen, train only a lightweight head, and ask whether Group SupCon + XBM + Radius improves retrieval over the same backbone. The second track reproduces the paper scenario with end-to-end ResNet-50 / 512-dim training.

IMDb transfer results remain secondary: they explain why cleaner embedding spaces can support smaller downstream classifiers, but the proposed method is judged first on image retrieval datasets.

Research question

Can group-aware supervision improve retrieval when we isolate the method in a simple frozen-backbone head experiment, and then when we reproduce paper-style ResNet-50 training?

Proposed answer

Use point SupCon plus same-class group centroids, then add cross-batch memory and radius control so groups stay discriminative and compact.

Evidence standard

Judge the power experiment with same-backbone MAP@R and Recall@1; judge the paper-protocol claim only with end-to-end ResNet-50/512 results.

Current result

Group SupCon + XBM + Radius wins 3/3 image datasets with a mean result gain of +1.3%.

Context

What existing metric learning misses

Standard triplet, SupCon, proxy, and margin-softmax losses mostly compare individual examples or class proxies. The missing piece is an objective that exposes group centroids, hard members, memory-backed negatives, and within-group radius at the same time.

Group SupCon + XBM + Radius

Combines point SupCon, group centroids, memory-backed negatives, and radius control in one projection-head objective. This is the method claimed in the headline and compared against the best same-backbone prior.

Same-class groups

Group SupCon keeps point SupCon and adds a same-class centroid term. The representative is a normalized group centroid. This preserves point-level positives while adding centroid movement and within-group structure before retrieval is evaluated.

Memory-backed negatives

Memory-backed negatives

XBM extends the comparison pool beyond the current mini-batch. Recent embeddings remain available as negatives. That makes the denominator harder and reduces the chance that a batch looks easy only because it missed confusing classes.

Radius-controlled neighborhoods

Radius regularization constrains how much each class-local group can spread. The head should improve ranking without creating brittle neighborhoods that are hard to reuse in downstream tasks.

SupCon, Group SupCon, and full recipe equations

These equations show the exact change in training unit. SupCon contrasts points. Group SupCon keeps that point term and adds normalized group centroids. The full proposed recipe adds memory plus radius control.

SupCon point loss

Training unit: individual examples.

Supervised Contrastive (SupCon) treats each example as the anchor and averages over same-class positive examples.

LSupCon (i)=- 1 |P(i)| ∑ p∈P(i) log exp(sim(zi,zp)/τ) ∑a∈A(i)exp(sim(zi,za)/τ)

Anchor, positives, and comparisons are all individual examples.

Group SupCon equation

Training unit: individual examples plus same-class groups.

Group SupCon keeps the point-level SupCon term and adds a supervised contrastive term over normalized group centroids.

μg= normalize ( 1 |g| ∑j∈g zj ) LGroupSupCon =α LSupCon (z) +γ LSupCon (μg)

The point term remains; the new term contrasts group centroids.

Full proposed recipe

Training unit: groups plus memory and radius.

The final method keeps point plus group SupCon, adds XBM hard-negative memory, and penalizes excessive group radius.

Lours= LGroupSupCon +βLXBM +ηLhard-group +λradius R(g)

The full recipe extends the Group SupCon core with XBM and radius control.

Interpretation

Evidence-backed interpretation

The conclusion is split into claim, caveat, and effect size so the report does not overstate one loss component or hide the role of XBM and radius regularization.

3/3datasets beat prior

Main claim

The full proposed recipe beats the best same-backbone non-proposed method on the image datasets reported here.

Corepoint plus group contrast

Method hierarchy

Group SupCon is the proposed core method: point SupCon plus normalized group-centroid contrast. The main recipe adds XBM and radius control on top.

+2.0%best result gain (Stanford Online Products)

Effect size

The percentage is computed from actual MAP@R results against the previous same-backbone method, so it reads as a direct result lift.

Final reading: the proposed method is Group SupCon + XBM + Radius. The measured gains come from the complete recipe: point-level contrast, group-level contrast, memory-backed comparisons, and radius control.

Limits

How to read the evidence

Primary evidence

The claim is based on held-out image retrieval: MAP@R, Recall@1, and result gain against the best same-backbone prior.

What is not compared

Objective losses are not used as cross-method evidence because each method optimizes a different loss surface.

Scope of the claim

The current validated claim covers frozen image encoders with projection-head training. The paper-level claim is pending ResNet-50/512 end-to-end results.
